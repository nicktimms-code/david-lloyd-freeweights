☀ Warm-up
Get Everything Moving
5 min
No rest — flow through continuously
1 Hip circles 10 each way ▾
  • Hands on hips, big slow circles
  • Go as wide as your range allows
  • Keep torso still — move from the hip socket
2 Bodyweight squats × 10 ▾
  • Feet shoulder width, toes slightly out
  • Sit back and down — aim to get hips below knees
  • Drive knees out over toes throughout
3 Glute bridges × 10 (2s hold) ▾
  • Feet flat, knees at 90°
  • Squeeze glutes hard at the top — hold 2 seconds
  • Tuck your pelvis (flatten lower back) at lockout
4 Inchworms × 5 ▾
  • Walk hands out to a plank, pause, walk feet to hands
  • Keep legs as straight as you can on the walk-in
  • Great for waking up hamstrings and shoulders
5 Light KB swings × 10 ▾
  • Use a light kettlebell — this is a warm-up, not a workout
  • Hinge at hips, not squatting — snap the hips forward
  • Arms are just ropes — power comes from the glutes

A1 Goblet squat 3 × 10 ▾
Start on 16kg KB
  • Hold KB or DB at chest height, elbows tucked in
  • Sit deep — hip crease below knee if possible
  • Push knees out wide at the bottom, pause for a beat
  • Drive up through the whole foot, squeezing glutes at top

Block 2 — Hinge + Press
≈7 min
Superset B1 ↔ B2 · Rest 60–90s between rounds · 3 rounds
B1 KB Romanian deadlift 3 × 10 ▾
Start on 16kg kettlebells
  • Hold two KBs in front of thighs, soft bend in knees
  • Push hips back like you're closing a car door with your bum
  • 3-second lowering — really feel the hamstring stretch
  • Stop when you feel maximum stretch, then drive hips forward
  • Keep KBs close to your legs the whole way

Block 3 — Split + Pull
≈8 min
Superset C1 ↔ C2 · Rest 60–90s between rounds · 3 rounds
C1 DB Bulgarian split squat 3 × 8/leg ▾
Start on 3kg dumbbells each side
  • Rear foot up on bench, shoelaces down
  • Front foot far enough forward that knee stays over ankle
  • Lean torso slightly forward to bias glutes over quads
  • Lower until back knee almost touches the floor
  • For the right hip — don't force depth if you feel a pinch

C2 Assisted pull-ups (weighted assistance) 3 × 6–8 ▾
Start on 180lbs assistance · Adjust arms out wider
  • Kneel on the pad, grip the handles with arms wider than shoulders
  • Palms facing away, pull elbows down and back — think "bend the bar"
  • Get chin clearly over the handles, lower fully each rep
  • Increase the weight to make it easier, decrease to make it harder
  • Control the descent — don't just drop back down

D1 Press-ups 3 × 8–12 ▾
  • Hands just wider than shoulders, fingers forward
  • Body in a straight line — no sagging hips or piking up

D2 Single arm cable mid row 3 × 10/arm ▾
Adjust height to just above 5 · Start on 14kg
  • Set cable at about belly button height
  • Square your hips — resist the rotation, that's the core work
  • Pull elbow straight back, squeeze shoulder blade in
  • Slow return — don't let the cable yank you forward

Becky's cues for the session: On all glute exercises, focus on the squeeze at the top and tuck your pelvis (posterior pelvic tilt). On RDLs and split squats, slow eccentrics — 3 seconds down. If the right hip objects to any movement, reduce depth or swap in an alternative below.
🔄 Alternatives

If Equipment Is Busy

Swap these in 1-for-1 if the cable machine or pull-up bar is occupied

DB bent-over row Swaps for: cable row ▾
  • One hand and knee on a bench for support
  • Pull DB to your hip, not your armpit
  • Keep your back flat and core braced throughout
  • Same anti-rotation benefit as the cable version
DB skull crushers Swaps for: cable pushdown ▾
  • Lie on bench, DBs above your face, arms straight
  • Bend at the elbow only — lower DBs to beside your ears
  • Keep elbows pointing at the ceiling, not flaring out
  • Use lighter weight than you think — this is an isolation move
Inverted body row (bar in rack) Swaps for: pull-ups ▾
  • Set a barbell in a rack at about waist height
  • Hang underneath, heels on floor, body straight
  • Pull chest to the bar, squeeze shoulder blades together
  • Walk feet further forward to make it harder
KB sumo deadlift Swaps for: KB RDL ▾
  • Wide stance, toes turned out 30–45°
  • KB hangs between your legs — sit hips back and down
  • Drive through heels, squeeze glutes hard at the top
  • More glute-dominant than a conventional stance
Sandbag bear hug squat Swaps for: goblet squat ▾
  • Hug the sandbag tight to your chest — arms wrapped around it
  • The instability forces extra core engagement
  • Squat as deep as you comfortably can
  • Great variation if all the KBs/DBs are taken
